Level 1 — Awareness

20 questions · Use for classroom discussion before or after the live quiz.

Session 1 Multiple choice

Q1. What is the primary purpose of ISO 9001:2015?

  1. To certify individual employees
  2. To provide requirements for a Quality Management System (Correct)
  3. To regulate manufacturing processes only
  4. To set financial auditing standards

    5. Discussion note: ISO 9001:2015 specifies requirements for a QMS helping organisations consistently meet customer and regulatory requirements.

Session 1 Multiple choice

Q2. How many quality management principles underpin ISO 9001:2015?

  1. 5
  2. 6
  3. 7 (Correct)
  4. 8

    5. Discussion note: ISO 9001:2015 is built on 7 principles: customer focus, leadership, engagement of people, process approach, improvement, evidence-based decision making, and relationship management.

Session 1 Multiple choice

Q3. Which clause covers Leadership and Commitment?

  1. Clause 4
  2. Clause 5 (Correct)
  3. Clause 6
  4. Clause 7

    5. Discussion note: Clause 5 covers Leadership. Top management must demonstrate leadership and commitment to the QMS.

Session 1 Multiple choice

Q4. What does context of the organisation (Clause 4) require?

  1. Determining headcount and budget
  2. Identifying internal and external issues relevant to the QMS (Correct)
  3. Benchmarking competitors only
  4. Measuring customer scores only

    5. Discussion note: Clause 4 requires determining external and internal issues relevant to the organisation that affect QMS outcomes.

Session 1 Multiple choice

Q5. Which is NOT one of the 7 QMS principles?

  1. Customer focus
  2. Zero defect manufacturing (Correct)
  3. Engagement of people
  4. Evidence-based decision making

    5. Discussion note: Zero defect manufacturing is not a principle. The 7 are: customer focus, leadership, engagement of people, process approach, improvement, evidence-based decision making, and relationship management.

Session 2 Multiple choice

Q6. What does PDCA stand for in ISO 9001:2015?

  1. Produce, Deliver, Check, Adjust
  2. Plan, Do, Check, Act (Correct)
  3. Plan, Deploy, Control, Audit
  4. Process, Document, Certify, Assess

    5. Discussion note: PDCA — Plan, Do, Check, Act — is a cyclical improvement method underpinning the entire structure of ISO 9001:2015.

Session 2 Multiple choice

Q7. What concept was formally introduced in ISO 9001:2015 not in 2008?

  1. Internal audits
  2. Risk-based thinking (Correct)
  3. Document control
  4. Management review

Session 2 Multiple choice

Q8. What does the process approach mean?

  1. Managing each department independently
  2. Managing interrelated activities as a system (Correct)
  3. Following steps in linear order only
  4. Outsourcing non-core processes

    5. Discussion note: The process approach means managing interrelated activities as a system, contributing to efficiency and effectiveness.

Session 3 Multiple choice

Q9. What does interested parties refer to in ISO 9001:2015?

  1. Shareholders and investors only
  2. Persons or organisations affected by the organisation's decisions (Correct)
  3. Certified suppliers only
  4. Internal employees only

    5. Discussion note: Interested parties are persons or organisations that can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by a decision.

Session 3 Multiple choice

Q10. Who is ultimately responsible for QMS effectiveness?

  1. Quality Manager only
  2. External consultants
  3. Top management (Correct)
  4. The certification body

    5. Discussion note: ISO 9001:2015 places accountability on top management who must demonstrate leadership and take responsibility for QMS effectiveness.

Session 4 Multiple choice

Q11. What does documented information cover in ISO 9001:2015?

  1. Only printed procedures
  2. Both documents to maintain and records to retain (Correct)
  3. Only quality records
  4. Documents approved by certifiers

    5. Discussion note: Documented information replaced documents and records. It includes information to be maintained (documents) and retained (records).

Session 4 Multiple choice

Q12. What is the purpose of a management review?

  1. To approve product designs
  2. To ensure the QMS remains suitable, adequate and effective (Correct)
  3. To conduct employee appraisals
  4. To plan marketing activities

    5. Discussion note: Management review (Clause 9.3) requires top management to review the QMS to ensure continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness.

Session 4 Multiple choice

Q13. Which clause covers Competence and Awareness?

  1. Clause 5
  2. Clause 6
  3. Clause 7 (Correct)
  4. Clause 8

    5. Discussion note: Clause 7 (Support) covers competence (7.2), awareness (7.3), communication (7.4), and documented information (7.5).

Session 5 Multiple choice

Q14. What is a corrective action in ISO 9001:2015?

  1. A preventive step before an issue
  2. An action to eliminate the cause of a nonconformity (Correct)
  3. A routine quality check
  4. A management review finding

    5. Discussion note: Corrective action (Clause 10.2) eliminates the root cause of a nonconformity to prevent recurrence.

Session 5 Multiple choice

Q15. What is continual improvement in ISO 9001:2015?

  1. Optional enhancement
  2. Applies only to manufacturers
  3. A core requirement to enhance QMS performance (Correct)
  4. Refers only to certification renewal

    5. Discussion note: Continual improvement (Clause 10.3) is fundamental — organisations must improve suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the QMS.

Session 5 Multiple choice

Q16. Which clause requires monitoring customer satisfaction?

  1. Clause 7
  2. Clause 8
  3. Clause 9 (Correct)
  4. Clause 10

Q17. What must be established as part of Clause 6 Planning?

  1. Profit targets and sales forecasts
  2. Quality objectives and plans to achieve them (Correct)
  3. Employee appraisal methods
  4. Customer feedback portals

    5. Discussion note: Clause 6 requires quality objectives at relevant functions and levels with plans covering resources, timelines, and evaluation.

Session 3 Multiple choice

Q18. What does Clause 8 Operations primarily address?

  1. Strategic planning and risk
  2. Planning and control of production and service provision (Correct)
  3. Internal audit procedures
  4. Employee training and competence

    5. Discussion note: Clause 8 covers operational planning and control — designing, producing, and delivering products and services that meet requirements.

Session 1 Multiple choice

Q19. What is the purpose of an internal audit under ISO 9001:2015?

  1. Prepare for certification renewal only
  2. Determine if QMS conforms and is effectively implemented (Correct)
  3. Evaluate competitor practices
  4. Replace management reviews

    5. Discussion note: Internal audits (Clause 9.2) determine whether the QMS conforms to ISO 9001:2015 requirements and is effectively implemented.

Session 2 Multiple choice

Q20. What is required when a nonconformity occurs under Clause 10.2?

  1. Immediate suspension of operations
  2. React, evaluate, determine root cause, take corrective action (Correct)

Level 2 — Practitioner

20 questions · Use for classroom discussion before or after the live quiz.

Session 1 Multiple choice

Q1. Which BEST describes risk-based thinking in ISO 9001:2015?

  1. Eliminating all risks from processes
  2. Considering risks and opportunities when planning the QMS (Correct)
  3. Conducting risk assessments only for critical products
  4. Applying risk tools only during management review

    5. Discussion note: Risk-based thinking means considering risks and opportunities when establishing, implementing and improving the QMS — integrated throughout.

Session 1 Multiple choice

Q2. What is the MINIMUM documented information required for internal audits per Clause 9.2?

  1. Audit programme only
  2. Evidence of audit programme and audit results (Correct)
  3. Full procedures and all checklists
  4. Signed statements from all auditees

    5. Discussion note: Clause 9.2.2 requires retaining documented information as evidence of the audit programme implementation and the audit results.

Session 2 Multiple choice

Q3. Which clause specifically requires determining the scope of the QMS?

  1. Clause 4.1
  2. Clause 4.2
  3. Clause 4.3 (Correct)
  4. Clause 5.1

    5. Discussion note: Clause 4.3 requires determining the boundaries and applicability of the QMS to establish its scope.

Session 2 Multiple choice

Q4. An organisation outsources calibration. Under ISO 9001:2015 what must it do?

  1. Exclude calibration from QMS scope
  2. Ensure control over externally provided processes (Correct)
  3. Simply audit the supplier annually
  4. Only check calibration certificates on receipt

    5. Discussion note: Clause 8.4 requires organisations to control externally provided processes — outsourcing does not remove responsibility for conformity.

Session 3 Multiple choice

Q5. What distinguishes maintaining from retaining documented information?

  1. No practical difference
  2. Maintaining = keep current (procedures); Retaining = keep as evidence (records) (Correct)
  3. Maintaining = digital; Retaining = paper
  4. Retaining applies only to customer records

    5. Discussion note: ISO 9001:2015 uses maintain for documents (living information) and retain for records (evidence of what happened, not to be altered).

Session 3 Multiple choice

Q6. Which BEST describes a quality objective?

  1. Any target set by top management
  2. A measurable goal consistent with quality policy, relevant to conformity (Correct)
  3. A KPI defined by the certification body
  4. Any improvement project target

    5. Discussion note: Clause 6.2 requires quality objectives to be measurable, consistent with quality policy, relevant to conformity, and monitored.

Session 4 Multiple choice

Q7. Under Clause 8.3 when can an organisation exclude Design and Development?

  1. When using only standard products
  2. When the nature of products/services makes design requirements inapplicable (Correct)
  3. When top management decides it unnecessary
  4. When the organisation has fewer than 50 employees

    5. Discussion note: Design and development can only be excluded when it can be justified — for example, a company that only resells without any design activity.

Session 4 Multiple choice

Q8. What does Clause 9.1.1 require regarding monitoring and measurement?

  1. Only customer satisfaction to be measured
  2. Determining what to monitor, when, using what methods, and when to analyse (Correct)
  3. Measuring all processes using statistical methods
  4. Monthly reporting of all KPIs to top management

    5. Discussion note: Clause 9.1.1 requires determining what needs monitoring and measuring, the methods needed, when to perform and analyse results.

Session 5 Multiple choice

  1. 2 — Plan and Do
  2. 3 — Plan, Do, Check
  3. 4 — Plan, Do, Check, Act (Correct)
  4. 5 — Plan, Design, Do, Check, Act

    5. Discussion note: The 4-stage PDCA maps to ISO 9001:2015: Plan (Clauses 4-6), Do (Clause 8), Check (Clause 9), Act (Clause 10).

Level 3 — Professional (Case Studies)

15 questions · Use for classroom discussion before or after the live quiz.

Session 1 Case study

Case: A manufacturing company produces automotive components. During an internal audit, the auditor finds the quality policy has never been communicated to shop floor workers. The Quality Manager argues only management needs to understand the policy.

Q1. Which clause is being violated and what is the most appropriate corrective action?

  1. Clause 5.2 — Post quality policy on notice boards and close the finding
  2. Clause 5.1 — Retrain the Quality Manager on leadership
  3. Clause 5.2 — Communicate quality policy to all persons, verify understanding, and make it available to interested parties (Correct)
  4. Clause 6.1 — Conduct a risk assessment on communication failures

    5. Discussion note: Clause 5.2.2 requires the quality policy to be communicated and understood within the organisation. Posting it is insufficient — verified understanding is required.

Session 2 Case study

Case: A certified software company receives a major contract to develop custom software. The project manager bypasses design and development controls (Clause 8.3) saying 'we use Agile so formal design review does not apply'.

Q2. Is the project manager's position correct under ISO 9001:2015?

  1. Yes — Agile is inherently compliant with ISO 9001:2015
  2. Yes — software development is excluded from Clause 8.3
  3. No — Clause 8.3 applies but can be adapted; Agile sprints can serve as design reviews with proper documentation (Correct)
  4. No — Agile methodology is not permitted under ISO 9001:2015

    5. Discussion note: Clause 8.3 applies to any organisation designing products or services. Sprint reviews, acceptance criteria, and retrospectives can satisfy design review, verification, and validation requirements with appropriate documented information.

Session 3 Case study

Case: During a supplier audit, a procurement officer discovers a key supplier has been providing non-conforming materials for 6 months. The supplier blames their subcontractor. The organisation has no documented supplier evaluation records for 2 years.

Q3. Which combination of clauses has been violated?

  1. Clause 8.4.1 only — evaluation records are missing
  2. Clause 8.4.1 and 8.4.3 — no re-evaluation and no communication of requirements
  3. Clause 8.4.1, 8.4.3, and 10.2 — no re-evaluation, no clear requirements, and no corrective action system (Correct)
  4. Clause 10.2 only — raise a corrective action

    5. Discussion note: Multiple clauses violated: 8.4.1 (no re-evaluation for 2 years), 8.4.3 (requirements not adequately communicated), and 10.2 (no corrective action despite 6 months of nonconforming inputs). Systemic failure requires systemic corrective action.

Session 4 Case study

Case: A hospital implements ISO 9001:2015. The external auditor raises a major nonconformity: the hospital cannot demonstrate it has identified all applicable statutory and regulatory requirements related to patient safety in its context analysis.

Q4. Which clause was cited and what is the most comprehensive corrective action?

  1. Clause 4.1 — Update the context document to list all regulations
  2. Clause 6.1 — Conduct a risk assessment on regulatory gaps
  3. Clause 4.1 combined with 8.2.2 — update context analysis AND establish a process to identify, monitor, and update all applicable legal requirements (Correct)
  4. Clause 9.1 — Improve monitoring and measurement

    5. Discussion note: Clause 4.1 requires determining external issues including regulatory requirements. Clause 8.2.2 requires determining applicable statutory requirements. The corrective action must establish a systematic process to capture, review, and communicate regulatory changes.

Session 5 Case study

Case: An internal auditor reviews the calibration programme and finds: 15 instruments have no calibration records, a critical gauge has been used 3 months past its due date, and the calibration procedure has not been reviewed in 5 years.

Q5. What is the CORRECT sequence of actions under ISO 9001:2015?

  1. Calibrate all instruments immediately and close the finding
  2. Raise a minor nonconformity and schedule calibration
  3. Raise a major nonconformity; quarantine the out-of-date gauge; assess validity of previous measurements; recalibrate; update the procedure (Correct)
  4. Only retrain the person responsible for calibration

    5. Discussion note: Major nonconformity against Clause 7.1.5. Critical actions: quarantine the gauge, assess impact on products measured with it (Clause 7.1.5.2(b)), calibrate all instruments, review and update the procedure, determine root cause and implement corrective action.

Session 1 Case study

Case: A food processing company is preparing for recertification. The Quality Manager presents a risk register created 3 years ago that has never been updated. Operations have changed significantly — new product lines, new suppliers, new markets.

Q6. Which principle and clause does this MOST directly violate?

  1. Clause 6.1 only — risks must be periodically reviewed
  2. Clause 4.1 and 6.1 — context changes must trigger risk register updates; risk is not a one-time exercise (Correct)
  3. Clause 9.3 — management review should have identified this gap
  4. Clause 10.3 — continual improvement requires updated risk tools

    5. Discussion note: Clause 4.1 requires monitoring and reviewing information about context. Clause 6.1 requires actions to address risks — this is a living process. When context changes significantly, the risk register must be updated.

Session 2 Case study

Case: During a management review, top management notices customer complaints increased 40% over 6 months. The Quality Manager presents corrective actions for individual complaints but there is no trend analysis or systemic root cause investigation.

Q7. What does ISO 9001:2015 require beyond individual corrective actions?

  1. Simply continue addressing individual complaints
  2. Analyse complaint data for trends, determine systemic root causes, and implement system-level improvements (Correct)
  3. Only survey customers to understand their concerns
  4. Immediately suspend production until complaints decrease

    5. Discussion note: Clause 9.1.3 requires analysis and evaluation of monitoring data including identifying trends. A 40% increase signals a systemic issue requiring trend analysis, root cause investigation at system level, and improvement actions.

Session 3 Case study

Case: A logistics company implementing ISO 9001:2015 decides to exclude Clause 8.3 (Design and Development) arguing they do not design products. However, they design complex delivery route optimisation systems and customised tracking software for clients.

Q8. Is this exclusion valid?

  1. Yes — logistics companies never design products
  2. Yes — software is not covered under Clause 8.3
  3. No — designing delivery systems and custom software constitutes design and development; the exclusion is invalid (Correct)
  4. No — all clauses are mandatory without exception

    5. Discussion note: Clause 4.3 permits exclusions only where requirements cannot be applied AND do not affect ability to provide conforming services. Designing route systems and custom software clearly constitutes design and development — the exclusion is invalid.

Session 4 Case study

Case: An internal audit reveals quality objectives for the production department were set two years ago and all achieved. No new objectives have been set. The production manager says we already meet all targets, why set new ones.

Q9. What does ISO 9001:2015 require in this situation?

  1. The production manager is correct — achieved objectives need not be replaced
  2. Quality objectives should be updated only when major changes occur
  3. New quality objectives must be set to drive continual improvement; achieving targets does not satisfy the continual improvement requirement (Correct)
  4. The audit finding should be closed since previous objectives were achieved

    5. Discussion note: Clause 6.2 requires quality objectives to be updated as needed. Clause 10.3 requires continual improvement. Achieving objectives is a starting point — organisations must set new, more challenging objectives to demonstrate genuine continual improvement.

Session 5 Case study

Case: A certification auditor finds competence records show all staff completed training 4 years ago with no subsequent evaluation of training effectiveness or skills reassessment despite new equipment and processes being introduced.

Q10. Which clauses are affected and what is the auditor most likely to raise?

  1. Clause 7.2 minor — training records exist so the clause is satisfied
  2. Clause 7.2 major — competence must be evaluated for effectiveness; records of new processes must be addressed (Correct)
  3. Clause 7.3 only — awareness training is missing
  4. Clause 8.1 — operations planning is affected

    5. Discussion note: Clause 7.2 requires determining necessary competence, ensuring persons are competent, evaluating effectiveness of actions, and retaining evidence. Having 4-year-old training records without effectiveness evaluation is a major nonconformity — competence must be maintained as the organisation changes.

Session 1 Case study

Case: During a surveillance audit, the auditor finds the organisation has a well-documented QMS but employees on the shop floor cannot explain the quality policy or their contribution to quality objectives when interviewed.

Q11. Which clauses are implicated and what corrective action addresses the root cause?

  1. Clause 5.2 only — communicate quality policy better
  2. Clause 7.3 only — conduct awareness training
  3. Clauses 5.2 and 7.3 — a systemic communication and awareness programme addressing root cause of why employees cannot articulate their role (Correct)
  4. Clause 8.1 — operations planning needs updating

    5. Discussion note: Both Clause 5.2 (quality policy communicated and understood) and Clause 7.3 (awareness of quality policy, objectives, and contribution) are implicated. Root cause must be determined — likely inadequate communication channels and onboarding — and a systemic programme implemented.

Session 2 Case study

Case: An organisation plans a significant expansion into a new market. The management team decides to update the quality policy and quality objectives but does not update the context analysis, risk register, or scope of the QMS.

Q12. What ISO 9001:2015 requirement has been overlooked and what are the potential consequences?

  1. Nothing — quality policy and objectives updates are sufficient
  2. Clause 4.1 and 4.3 — context analysis and scope must be reviewed when significant changes occur; the QMS may not address new risks (Correct)
  3. Clause 9.3 — management review must approve the expansion
  4. Clause 6.1 — only risk assessment needs updating

    5. Discussion note: Clause 4.1 requires monitoring and reviewing context. Clause 4.3 requires scope to reflect applicable products/services. Entering a new market changes external context, risks, interested parties, and potentially scope — all must be reviewed before the QMS can effectively govern the new operations.

Session 3 Case study

Case: A nonconformity is raised because the organisation cannot provide evidence that customer requirements were reviewed and confirmed before accepting an order. The sales team argues this happens verbally and does not need documentation.

Q13. Is the sales team correct? What does Clause 8.2.3 require?

  1. Yes — verbal review is acceptable if the team confirms it happened
  2. No — Clause 8.2.3 requires retaining documented information on results of review and any new requirements (Correct)
  3. Yes — only complex orders require documented review
  4. No — all requirements must be reviewed by the quality department only

    5. Discussion note: Clause 8.2.3 requires reviewing customer requirements before committing to supply products/services AND retaining documented information on the results of the review and any new or changed requirements. Verbal review with no records does not satisfy this requirement.

Session 4 Case study

Case: An organisation's customer audit team finds that the organisation tracks on-time delivery but has never measured customer satisfaction through surveys, feedback analysis, or any other method. The Quality Manager argues delivery performance is sufficient evidence.

Q14. Does on-time delivery data alone satisfy Clause 9.1.2?

  1. Yes — on-time delivery directly reflects customer satisfaction
  2. Yes — if no complaints are received, satisfaction can be assumed
  3. No — Clause 9.1.2 requires monitoring customer perception specifically and organisations must determine methods to obtain that information (Correct)
  4. No — only formal surveys satisfy the clause

    5. Discussion note: Clause 9.1.2 specifically requires monitoring customer perception — how customers perceive the organisation's products and services. This must be actively obtained through surveys, feedback, compliments, warranty data, or other methods. Absence of complaints does not constitute monitoring of perception.

Session 5 Case study

Case: An organisation implements ISO 9001:2015 and achieves certification. Three years later during recertification, the auditor finds that while all documents are updated, the actual processes on the shop floor do not match the documented procedures, and staff follow their own informal methods.

Q15. Which fundamental QMS failure does this represent and which clauses are violated?

  1. Clause 7.5 only — documented information is not controlled
  2. Clauses 4.4, 7.5, and 8.1 — the QMS is documented but not implemented; processes are not controlled as planned (Correct)
  3. Clause 9.2 — internal audits should have caught this
  4. Clause 5.1 — leadership has not demonstrated commitment

    5. Discussion note: This represents a fundamental implementation failure. Clause 4.4 requires processes to be implemented and maintained as planned. Clause 8.1 requires control of planned processes. Clause 7.5 requires documented information to reflect actual practice. Certification based on paper compliance without actual implementation is a systemic failure requiring complete reassessment.
